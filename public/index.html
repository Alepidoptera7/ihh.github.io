Holmes Lab

Computational Biology Research

Genomes are a living record of ecological history, and an open frontier for engineering and medicine. Our UC Berkeley lab makes the software that allows scientists to study, interpret, and shape this world.

We develop algorithms and tools for genome informatics, using techniques from Bayesian statistics and deep learning through to modern web development. We’re particularly interested in applications to cancer genomics, virus evolution, and protein engineering.

Our group is a mix of academics and research software engineers, working on multiple projects. We develop new algorithms and models, gather data on natural and synthetic sequences, and develop code to visualize and explore data.
